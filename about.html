About Me

Researcher.
Builder. Defender.

CS graduate student at Texas State University — pushing the boundary between AI systems research and enterprise security engineering.

I'm a Computer Science senior and graduate student at Texas State University (GPA 3.6, graduating May 2026), with a dual focus that most people don't see together: GPU-accelerated AI systems and enterprise Identity & Access Management.

My research, under Professor Apan Qasem, centers on GPULLM — an AI-driven CUDA memory placement recommendation system that uses LangChain and CodeLlama to automatically optimize HPC workloads on Texas State's Ada cluster. The project achieves 100% accuracy across six HeCBench kernels.

Professionally, I've spent 2+ years at BNY Mellon as an IAM Administrator, managing access lifecycle, provisioning workflows, and access reviews using SailPoint IdentityIQ v8.1 — for which I hold an official certification.

I also completed a software development internship at Apple, sharpening my engineering fundamentals in a world-class production environment.
